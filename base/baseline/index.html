Introduction

Baseline is an implementation of Compass's Vertical Rhythm. In the context of Persistent Grid it is renamed to "Baseline" because the term is more compatible with traditional Graphic Design, where a baseline is the imaginary line that touches the bottom of the letter 'x'.

There are some differences between the implementation of a baseline in print and in webdesign though. One such difference is that in webdesign, the text does not sit 'on' the baseline, but rather sits 'in-between' baselines. Another difference addresses images. In print, images would also be aligned with the baseline (with the bottom of the image sitting on the baseline and the top of the image aligned with the top of foresaid letter 'x').

Because of a website's flexible nature, aligning images in a similar way is impractical. Therefore the implementation of a baseline and derivative vertical measures like paddings, margins and borders are merely introduced to convey a sense of order to a webpage.
